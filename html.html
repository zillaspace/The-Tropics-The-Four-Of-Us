This is v0.2. A starting place. Not a position, not a proposal, not a thing that's been decided behind anyone's back. It's a working draft so the four of us have something concrete to push against in a real conversation.

03 — How We Work Together, For Now

Light framework. Heavy on goodwill.

A starting view of who leads what in the early phase. Not a job description and not a fence. The point is to give us a shared way to talk about decisions, not to put us in lanes.

The four of us together. Annual budget. Capital structure changes. Brand affiliations or management agreements. Asset sale or material refi. Key hires above department head. Anything that meaningfully changes how a guest experiences the property. Anything material we don't already have a view on. These come to the four of us.

Raymond. Chairman of the partnership. Casting vote in the unlikely event of true deadlock. Senior LP and lender relationships. The credibility anchor when we need it.

Michelle. Owns the capital story end to end. Underwriting, lender relationships, asset management oversight, refi prep, investor reporting. Co leads operating performance against the underwriting with Michael.

Michael. Owns the property through pre opening, renovation, and stabilization. Hires and leads the senior hotel team. Hands day to day off to the GM when the asset is stable and the right operator is in seat.

Mark. Owns brand, guest experience, programming, marketing, and creative production. Sets standards that hold once the GM is running the day to day.

Disagreements get talked through, not voted on. The four of us being in the room together is the governance mechanism. Everything else is logistics.

04 — Three Horizons

This is the figuring it out period. The refi is the test.

Roles in this document describe how we're starting. The shape of the partnership in Year 1 is different from Year 3 is different from Year 5. Worth saying out loud.

Now to Year 1

Figuring it out.

Acquisition, renovation, pre opening. Michael full time on property. Michelle running diligence, financing, and asset management setup. Mark leading brand build and programming launch. Raymond on senior counsel. We are testing what each of us actually carries best, and finding the texture of how we work together. Everything in this document is most accurate for this horizon.

Year 1 to 3

Hiring the GM. Stabilizing.

A General Manager is hired to run day to day operations. Michael's full time presence tapers as the senior hotel team comes into form. Mark's role shifts from active build to brand standards and oversight. Michelle increases focus on asset management and refi preparation. Raymond steps back from any day to day. The four of us move toward board and asset management level engagement.

Year 3 to 4

The refinance. The real test.

The asset is stabilized. The refi happens against near stabilized cash flows and returns substantial LP capital. The four of us are operating at strategic and board level. The GM and senior hotel team run the property. This is the moment we know whether the partnership and the asset have done what we underwrote. The roles described earlier are largely behind us.

Read every section that follows with this in mind. We are designing for the figuring it out period, not the steady state.
05 — Raising The 49%

Sourcing the LP tranche. Without crossing wires.

Mark and Michael have been asked to lead sourcing the LP equity. Here's a working view of how that runs alongside the institutional IR work Michelle leads at and after close.

Goal: roughly $2.6M in LP equity, substantively closed within 90 days, with signed commitment letters by end of due diligence. Confidence over urgency. Runway exists; we do not advertise it.

Every investor is the partnership's investor. Every investor also has a relationship owner — whoever sourced them and holds the trust. The relationship owner stays the primary point of contact for the life of the deal, even when the conversation needs skills they don't carry.

How We Avoid Crossed Wires

How it works. Mark and Michael source. They hold the personal relationship for life. Michelle runs the institutional side — diligence, structuring, close, quarterly reporting, refi communications. Originator brings Michelle in for the right conversations, stays in the room, and remains the investor's first call. Raymond engages on senior moments.

Where we go for the money. Three channels in parallel. Direct is fastest: personal HNW network, past LBHG and Comporta LPs, Las Brisas relationships, family referrals, anchor conversations with Doug, Dominique, Bob Best. Institutional brings larger checks but takes longer: single and multi family offices, hospitality investment funds, strategic operators, Michelle's Marriott and Starwood alumni network. Intermediated widens reach at a fee: placement agents at 3 to 3.5 percent, hospitality brokers like Hodges Ward Elliott or RobertDouglas or JMBM.

The anchor strategy. One large committed check early in the window changes the gravity of every other conversation. First priority is a single signature at $1M or above. Doug, Dominique, and the Comporta pool are the strongest near term candidates. Bob Best is tested early so the equity story doesn't depend on his answer.

If Michelle, Michael, or Raymond have investor relationships they'd like to bring, the relationship owner principle applies to them just as it does to Mark and Michael. The point is no investor is anyone's possession; the point is no investor experiences crossed wires.
06 — Pre Close Costs

The costs to reach close. How we treat them.

Michelle and Mark are covering the legal and diligence costs to reach close. Raymond is writing the $650K GP cash. Different forms of risk to reach the same close. Here's a working view of how the pre close costs flow back through.

Pre close costs include PSA and operating agreement legal, BIA counsel, SBA lender legal and packaging, environmental and physical diligence, accounting and tax, operating model professional fees, and travel. Likely range $75K to $200K+ across the diligence window. If the deal dies, these are gone. If the deal closes, they get treated one of these ways.

Lens 01 — Goodwill

No payback. Treated as a gift to the deal. Simple, but transfers real risk from the partnership to two principals without any acknowledgment, and that's not how we want to operate going forward.

A starting view

Lens 02 — Recoupable at 1.25x

Documented as Pre Close Advance Notes. Reimbursed at close at 1.25x the advanced amount, before any equity distribution. If the deal dies, the advance is lost in full. The 1.25x is a modest recognition that two principals are absorbing 100 percent of the downside if the deal fails to close. Clean to administer, fair on both sides, normal for partner advanced capital, and easy to point at if anyone asks.

Lens 03 — Reimbursement at par

Same mechanic, no premium. Cleanest possible version. If the family prefers no premium, this works too and we move on.

All of this is open. The point is to have one written view of how it could work, not to settle it in advance of a real conversation.
